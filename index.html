Personas & Storyboarding

Interface: Dorm Kitchen Microwave

Interview Questions

  1. How often do you use the microwave?
  2. What do you normally cook in the microwave?
  3. Do you think you could explain how to operate the microwave to someone who has never seen or used one before?
  4. What is the most difficult part of operating the microwave?
  5. If there was one feature you could change or add to the microwave what would it be?

Key Observations

  • Most users would place the food item they were heating in the microwave, close the door, and then take a second to take a closer look at the interface before entering the desired cook time or mode.
  • Some users seemed startled / surprised when they pressed one of the numbers, in the “express cook” section, and the microwave immediately started with the cook time set to the number of minutes pressed.
  • When the food was done and the alarm went off most users were quick to open the microwave door to stop the sound that signaled the time was up

Interview Response Summary

  • How often do you use the microwave?

    How often individuals used the microwave ranged from around once a day to once a month, with the majority of people using the microwave a few times a week.

  • What do you normally cook in the microwave?

    What people cooked in the microwave varied, but were typical microwavable foods. Example responses were ramen, microwavable meals, and popcorn.

  • Do you think you could explain how to operate the microwave to someone who has never seen or used one before?

    Most people said they could explain how to operate a microwave easily.

  • What is the most difficult part of operating the microwave?

    People found setting the cook time the most difficult part of operating a microwave.

  • If there was one feature you could change or add to the microwave what would it be?

    People wanted a variety of different features, ranging from a ramen button to a silent mode (for when the alarm goes off), but almost all the answers related to accessibility for the user.
